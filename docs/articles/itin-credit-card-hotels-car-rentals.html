Using Your ITIN Credit Card for Hotels & Car Rentals

By Editorial Staff · Secured & Starter Cards · Published July 24, 2026

Quick Answer

Yes. Any credit card accepted by a hotel or car rental company works regardless of whether you applied with an ITIN or an SSN. Hotels and rental agencies check available credit, not your tax ID. The key is having enough available credit to cover the authorization hold, typically $100-$500.

Your ITIN credit card works exactly like any other Visa, Mastercard, or Amex at a hotel front desk or rental car counter. The agency sees a valid card on an open network, not the tax ID you used to apply. That said, a few practical things specific to ITIN holders, especially around authorization holds and credit limits, are worth knowing before you arrive at the counter.

Does my ITIN credit card actually work at hotel check-in?

Yes, completely.

Hotels place a temporary authorization hold for incidentals, damage, or a security deposit without charging your account immediately. That process happens at the card network level and has nothing to do with how you applied. The desk agent swipes your card, the network approves or declines based on available credit, and you check in.

The one thing that matters: your name on the card must match your passport or photo ID. This is standard fraud prevention that applies to every guest, not just ITIN holders. Keep your card and your primary ID consistent.

Authorization holds exist because the hotel wants to confirm a valid payment method before handing over a room key. Additional deposits are often required beyond the anticipated cost of the stay to cover things like room service charges or incidental damage. Plan for that before you arrive.

How much available credit do I need for a hotel stay?

A question we hear often: ITIN holders with newer secured cards sometimes get surprised when a hold eats up most of their available credit.

Most hotels place a per-night incidental hold on top of your room rate. Budget chains may hold $50-$100 per night; mid-scale and upscale properties commonly hold $150-$250 per night. A three-night stay at a mid-range hotel could temporarily tie up $450-$750 in addition to your room charges. Some properties authorize the card at the start of your stay, and that block on your available credit stays in place throughout, not just at checkout.

If your ITIN secured card has a $200 limit, you are likely to be declined at check-in simply because the hold would exceed your available credit. The hotel doesn’t know or care about your ITIN. The fix is straightforward: request a credit limit increase before you travel (see our guide on how to get a credit limit increase with an ITIN credit card), or carry a second card as a backup. Aim to have at least $600-$800 in available credit before booking a multi-night stay.

After checkout, expect 3-7 business days for the hold to drop off, depending on your bank.

Can I rent a car with a credit card I got using my ITIN?

Readers frequently ask whether rental companies have a separate ID check that would flag an ITIN card.

They do not. Car rental agencies identify you with your passport and driver’s license, then take payment and a security deposit using an accepted card. An SSN is primarily a U.S. employment and credit identifier, not a standard requirement for short-term rentals. The rental company’s system simply authorizes your card for the estimated total plus a deposit buffer.

Many suppliers require a hold of at least $200, and in some cases they may hold the full estimated rental charges if those exceed $200. Some Thrifty locations in the United States, for example, hold the estimated rental amount plus an additional 15%. Always confirm the specific hold amount with your rental company before pickup.

One hard rule: prepaid cards or any non-credit cards without a Visa, Mastercard, or Discover logo are not accepted by most major rental agencies. A genuine credit card obtained with your ITIN passes this test without issue.

What about using a debit card if my ITIN credit card limit is still low?

This one comes up a lot, especially from readers who just got their first secured card and are wondering whether their debit card is a fallback.

Debit cards are technically accepted by some rental agencies, but the rules are significantly stricter. Debit card deposits are only accepted at the time of rental if accompanied by a ticketed return travel itinerary. Without one, you’ll need a credit card with enough available credit to cover the estimated total and a deposit of $200-$850, depending on the rental location and vehicle class.

There’s also a cash flow problem with debit. Unlike a credit card hold, a debit card authorization immediately reduces your available bank balance, and that hold can take 7-15 business days to clear after the car is returned. That can leave you short on cash during the trip itself.

The practical takeaway: grow your ITIN credit card’s limit to at least $800-$1,000 before relying on it for car rentals. That’s a realistic target after 6-12 months of responsible use. Upgrading from a secured to an unsecured card (see our guide on how to upgrade from secured to unsecured credit card with an ITIN) typically comes with a meaningful limit increase that makes travel much less stressful.

What documents should I bring to the hotel or rental counter?

Readers frequently ask whether immigration documents or their ITIN assignment letter need to come along. They do not.

For a hotel check-in, bring:

  • Your credit card (matching the name on your reservation)
  • A government-issued photo ID (passport is universally accepted)
  • Your reservation confirmation number

For a car rental, bring:

  • Your credit card with enough available credit for the hold
  • Your passport
  • A valid driver’s license from your home country or a U.S. state license
  • Your reservation confirmation

A passport, driver’s license, and credit card cover everything at the counter. Your ITIN number, immigration documents, and IRS assignment letter stay at home.

One practical note from our editorial team: use the same card for the reservation and for check-in or pickup. Many rental agencies will decline a different card at pickup even if the original booking went through. This also means you need the available credit headroom on that specific card, not spread across multiple cards.

Frequently asked questions

Do hotels check whether I used an ITIN or SSN to get my credit card? No. Hotels only verify that your card is valid, has sufficient available credit, and matches your name on the ID you present at check-in. Your tax ID number is never part of that process.

How large is a typical hotel authorization hold on a credit card? Most hotels place a hold of $50-$200 per night for incidentals, on top of your room charges. Budget properties often hold less; luxury hotels can hold $300 or more per night. The hold is released within 3-7 business days after checkout.

Can I rent a car with a secured ITIN credit card? Yes, but your credit limit must cover both the rental charge and the deposit hold, which can be $200-$500 or more. A secured card with only a $200 limit may not leave enough headroom. Aim for a card with at least a $500-$1,000 limit before renting.

Will a prepaid card or debit card work if I don’t have a regular ITIN credit card yet? Most major rental companies (Enterprise, Hertz, Avis) do not accept prepaid cards and have strict debit card rules that often require a credit check or proof of return travel. A true credit card obtained with your ITIN is the cleanest option.

Which ITIN-friendly credit cards earn the most rewards on hotel and car rental bookings? Capital One cards (Quicksilver Secured, QuicksilverOne, Savor Student) earn 5% cash back on hotels, vacation rentals, and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel. The American Express Gold Card, also available with an ITIN, earns strong points on travel and dining.
