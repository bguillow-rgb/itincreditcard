Expired ITIN and Your Credit Card: What Happens (2026)

By Editorial Staff · Secured & Starter Cards · Published July 9, 2026

Advertiser disclosure:ITIN Credit Card is an independent resource. Some links on this page are from partners who compensate us, which may affect which products we feature and where. This does not change our editorial guidance and is never a cost to you. See our full disclosure.

Quick Answer

Your existing credit card accounts stay open and keep reporting to all three bureaus even if your ITIN expires. However, applying for a new card with an expired ITIN will likely be declined at identity verification. Renew your ITIN using IRS Form W-7 before you need a new card, and your credit history remains fully intact.

Most ITIN holders only discover the gap when it’s too late: they apply for a new credit card, get flagged at identity verification, and receive a denial before an underwriter has looked at their credit file. The culprit is usually an expired ITIN they didn’t know had lapsed. This guide explains exactly what an ITIN expiration does (and doesn’t) affect when it comes to your credit cards, and how to protect your position.

Wait, can an ITIN actually expire? I thought it was permanent.

Yes, ITINs can and do expire, and many holders are unaware until a problem surfaces.

SSNs do not expire or need to be renewed, but ITINs can expire if they are not used on a federal tax return at least once every three years. Specifically, an ITIN expires after three consecutive years of non-use, on December 31 of the third year. There is also a batch-expiration rule from older issuances: ITINs with middle digits 90, 91, 92, and 94-99 issued before 2013 expired in IRS batch rounds between 2016 and 2020.

The practical implication for cardholders is straightforward. If you received your ITIN several years ago, filed taxes for a couple of years, then stopped filing because your income changed or your situation shifted, your ITIN may have quietly expired on December 31 of the third consecutive non-filing year. You wouldn’t necessarily get a warning. Most people find out their ITIN expired only when the IRS quietly rejects their tax credit or sends them a letter they did not expect: the CP48 notice.

Does an expired ITIN close my existing credit card accounts?

This is the most important question for cardholders, and the answer is a firm no.

Banks verify ITINs at the time of account opening, not annually, so an expired ITIN may still work for banking purposes. Credit card issuers don’t run periodic identity re-verification checks against IRS records. Once your account is open and in good standing, an ITIN expiration has no effect on the account itself. Your card keeps working, your payments keep being reported to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion, and your credit history keeps accumulating.

Renewal keeps your same ITIN number. Your credit history, tax records, and financial accounts linked to your ITIN all remain intact. Only the active or expired status changes. Expiration is an IRS status, not a financial system status. Your creditors and the credit bureaus don’t receive automatic notification that your ITIN has lapsed.

Don’t panic about losing your open credit card accounts. They’re safe.

So what actually gets blocked when my ITIN expires?

This because the consequences are more targeted than most people expect. An expired ITIN creates two specific credit card problems.

First, new credit card applications will likely fail. When you submit a new application, issuers run a Know Your Customer (KYC) identity verification as required by federal law. Banks and credit card issuers can legally accept an ITIN under the Patriot Act’s Know Your Customer rules. That verification process includes checking that the identifying number you provided is valid and active. An expired ITIN will fail this check for most issuers, resulting in a denial at the identity stage before your credit file is ever meaningfully reviewed.

Second, credit limit increase requests that involve a fresh identity check may be blocked. Some issuers, particularly when a requested increase is large, run new identity verification. If your ITIN is expired when you submit that request, it can be declined for the same reason as a new application.

Everything else, including your existing card’s spending, rewards, automatic payments, and credit bureau reporting, continues without interruption.

How does ITIN expiration affect my credit history and credit score?

Especially from ITIN holders who have spent 12-24 months carefully building a U.S. credit file.

Here is what many people do not realize: credit bureaus can create and maintain credit files using an ITIN. When you open a credit account with an ITIN and make payments, the lender reports that activity to the credit bureaus, just like they would for someone with an SSN. Over time, you build a credit history, and eventually, you get a credit score.

That file lives at the bureau level, not the IRS level. An expired ITIN doesn’t erase or freeze your bureau file. Your payment history, account ages, and credit utilization data all remain on file as normal, and your credit score is calculated from those bureau records, not from IRS data.

The indirect risk is behavioral. If you can’t open a new card or increase a limit because of an expired ITIN, your credit utilization could creep up on existing cards, which can nudge your score downward. That’s why renewing promptly matters, even if your current accounts are fine. For more on how card activity connects to your score, see our guide to building credit with an ITIN credit card.

How do I renew my ITIN, and how long will it take?

Renewal uses the same form as the original application. Here is a quick reference:

Renewal MethodProcessing Time (2026)CostDocuments Returned?
Mail to IRS (Austin, TX)8-14 weeksFreeNo (originals kept briefly)
Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA)7-11 weeks$50-$200 agent feeYes, immediately
IRS Taxpayer Assistance Center (TAC)8-14 weeksFreeYes, at appointment
Peak season (Jan-Apr) any method16-20 weeksSame as aboveVaries

Renewal requires Form W-7 submitted on paper. Processing takes about 7 weeks under standard conditions, and 9 to 11 weeks during peak filing season or for applications sent from abroad.

The IRS does not charge a fee to renew an ITIN. If you use a Certifying Acceptance Agent, the CAA may charge a service fee, typically $50 to $200 depending on the agent.

For the renewal package, you need a completed Form W-7 with the “Renew an existing ITIN” box checked, plus identity and foreign status documentation. The IRS accepts 13 types of identity documents for Form W-7 requirements, with a valid passport being the only document accepted on its own. No passport available? Submit at least two other approved documents.

For most ITIN holders, the CAA route is the safest option: the agent verifies your passport in person and returns it immediately, so your original document is never mailed. If you plan to apply for a new credit card or request a limit increase, factor the renewal timeline in and submit your W-7 at least 3-4 months before you need the active ITIN.

Will I lose my credit history if I switch from my ITIN to an SSN later?

No, and this is covered in detail in our dedicated guide on what happens to your credit cards when you get an SSN after using an ITIN. The short version: your credit history transfers with you. If an individual later obtains an SSN, it is possible to merge ITIN-based credit history with the new SSN credit file by contacting the major credit bureaus directly. Your account ages, payment records, and scores don’t reset.

The key action is to notify each of the three bureaus in writing and update your identifying number with each card issuer. Don’t let both numbers run simultaneously on active accounts because that can create a mixed-file problem.

What should I do right now if I think my ITIN might be expired?

For a clear action checklist, so here it is:

  1. Check your ITIN’s middle digits. Look at any old tax return or your original IRS letter. If your middle digits fall in the 70-99 range and you received the ITIN before 2013, it has very likely already expired under the batch schedule.
  2. Apply the three-year non-use test. If your ITIN was not included on at least one federal tax return during a three-year period, it automatically expires on December 31 of that third year. Count backwards from the last year you filed.
  3. Don’t apply for a new credit card yet. If you suspect expiration, confirm status first. A denial for identity reasons still generates a hard inquiry on your file in many cases, which can temporarily lower your credit score. Wait until your renewal is confirmed.
  4. Choose your renewal method and submit immediately. Outside of tax season (May through December), standard mail to the IRS Austin center is fine. During January through April, use a CAA for faster processing.
  5. Keep your existing cards active during the wait. Use each card for at least one small purchase per month and pay the balance in full. This keeps your accounts from being flagged as dormant and maintains positive reporting throughout the renewal period.
  6. Apply for the new card after your renewed ITIN letter arrives. Once the IRS issues your renewed number (same digits, new active status), you’re clear to apply. Consider pre-qualifying with issuers that use a soft pull before submitting a full application.

Common questions

Will my current credit card be closed if my ITIN expires?

No. Card issuers verify your identity at the time of application, not on an ongoing basis. An ITIN expiration does not trigger an automatic account closure. Your card stays open, continues to report payment history to the credit bureaus, and works normally for purchases.

Can I apply for a new credit card with an expired ITIN?

Almost certainly not. Most issuers run an identity verification check during the application process. An expired ITIN will fail that check, resulting in a denial before an underwriter even reviews your credit file. Renew your ITIN first, then apply.

Does my credit history disappear if my ITIN expires?

No. Credit bureaus store your history under your ITIN number indefinitely. Expiration only affects IRS tax processing, not your credit file. When you renew, your ITIN number stays the same, so every account and payment record linked to it remains untouched.

How long does ITIN renewal take in 2026?

Standard mail processing takes 7-14 weeks. Filing through a Certifying Acceptance Agent (CAA) is typically faster at 7-11 weeks. Submitting between January and April during peak tax season can push wait times to 16-20 weeks, so renew early.

Does ITIN renewal cost anything?

The IRS charges no fee to renew an ITIN. If you use a Certifying Acceptance Agent to handle the paperwork, the CAA may charge a service fee, typically in the range of $50-$200 depending on the agent.

What happens to my credit card credit limit during the ITIN renewal waiting period?

Nothing changes on your existing accounts. Your credit limit, interest rate, and reporting cycle are unaffected during renewal. The only action blocked is opening a new account or requesting a credit limit increase that requires a new identity verification check.
